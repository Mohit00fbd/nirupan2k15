JAM (Just A Minute)

The object of the game is for participant to talk for sixty seconds on a given subject(provided on the spot), without hesitation, repetition or deviation. The comedy comes from attempts to keep within these rules and the banter among the participants.

Rules

The panellists are invited, in rotation, to speak for one minute on a given subject (which they will not be told in advance), without hesitation, repetition or deviation.

  • Hesitation watched very strictly: a momentary pause in speaking can give rise to a successful challenge, as can tripping over one's words. Even pausing during audience laughter or applause would be challenged.
  • Repetition means the repetition of any word or phrase, although challenges based upon very common words such as and.
  • Deviation is meant for deviating from the given subject, but also include "deviating from the English language as we know it", "deviation from grammar as we understand it", and deviating from logic. Nevertheless, leaps into the surreal are allowed.

The game shall be conducted by an arbitrator.
